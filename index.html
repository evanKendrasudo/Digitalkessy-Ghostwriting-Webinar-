Path one — understand this first

What is Authoring?

Authoring simply means: you write stories under your own name. You are the author. You create a story — a billionaire romance, a mafia love story, a werewolf series — and you publish it on a platform like Dreame, GoodNovel, or WebNovel.

Readers find your book. They start reading. When they reach a locked chapter, they pay to unlock it. Every time someone pays to read your chapter — you earn. Every reader who loves your work becomes a fan who follows you, shares your story, and waits for the next chapter.

Beyond reader income, platforms also offer you bonuses. A signing bonus when your story gets accepted ($50–$200+). A daily bonus when you post consistently. A completion bonus when you finish your book ($50–$500+). And monthly performance rewards based on how your story is doing.

The income from authoring takes time to grow — but it compounds. A story you wrote months ago can still be earning while you sleep. You build your name, your audience, your legacy. And once readers love you, they follow every new book you release.

This is how I earned $127 from Gemstone Novel, 5,000 ink from Mespery, and over $1,200 in total revenue — from stories I wrote and published under my own name.

Path two — the fast income skill

What is Ghostwriting?

HOW GHOSTWRITING WORKS
A client hires you and gives you the story idea or genre
You write the full chapters — they tell you the direction
When done, they publish it under their name
You receive payment — per 1,000 words or per project
No waiting for readers. No building audience. Cash directly.
REAL EXAMPLE
A client pays $4 per 1,000 words. You write a 15,000-word short novel = $60 upfront plus a signing bonus. Rate increases as you prove yourself. Some ghostwriters earn $10–$20+ per 1,000 words.

Ghostwriting means someone hires you to write a story for them. They have an idea, a genre, a story they want written — but they either don't have the skill, the time, or the confidence to write it themselves. So they pay you to do it.

You write it. You hand it over. They publish it under their own name. You never get the credit — and that is perfectly fine. Because what you get is money. Direct. Fast. No waiting.

Ghostwriting is not who you are. It is a skill you use. A tool in your hand. You can ghostwrite for a client this week and publish your own story next week. The two do not compete — they work together.

The beauty of ghostwriting is that it gives you income right now, while your author career is still growing. You don't need thousands of readers. You just need one client who sees your skill — and they will pay you for it.

Ghostwriting is not selling yourself. It is using your talent strategically while you build your empire on the side.

The smart move? Build your name as an author — and ghostwrite on the side when you need cash now. Both paths are valid. Both pay real money.

Two ways to earn from writing

Build your name.
Or earn right now.

Most people don't realise there are two completely different ways to make money as a writer. You don't have to choose just one — but you need to understand both.

✍️
What is Authoring?

Authoring means you write stories under your own name. You publish on platforms like Dreame, GoodNovel, or WebNovel — and readers follow you. Every chapter you publish builds your reputation. Every reader who loves your work becomes a fan of your brand.

The income takes time to grow — but it compounds. Platforms pay you based on how many readers unlock your chapters, how long they stay, and how many words you complete. When you sign a contract, you get a signing bonus. When you finish a book, you get a completion bonus. When your readers keep reading, you earn every single month.

This is how I earned $1,216 in total revenue — and $116 in a single month — from one serialized novel.

  • Your name. Your story. Your brand.
  • Signing bonus + completion bonus + reader income
  • Build an audience that follows you long-term
  • You own everything you write
or
🖊️
What is Ghostwriting?

Ghostwriting means you write stories for someone else. They hire you, give you the idea or genre, and you write the chapters. When it's done — they publish it under their name. You never get the credit.

But here's what you do get: paid immediately. No waiting for readers. No building an audience. A client pays you per 1,000 words — sometimes $4, sometimes $10, sometimes more — and you move to the next project. It is not who you are. It is a skill you use to earn while your own author brand grows.

Real ghostwriting clients offer $4/1,000 words to start — rising as you prove yourself. A 15k-word novel = $60 upfront, plus signing bonus. That's cash in hand, fast.

  • You write — they publish under their name
  • You get paid directly, per word or per project
  • No audience needed. No waiting.
  • A backup income that runs alongside your author career

The smart move? Build your name as an author — and ghostwrite on the side when you need cash now.
